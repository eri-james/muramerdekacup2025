M.U.R.A. Freedom Cup 2025

Event Planning Documentation

This document outlines the planning and execution strategy for the Malayan Umamusume Racing Association (M.U.R.A.) Freedom Cup 2025, a triple elimination in-game tournament for Malaysian and Singaporean players. The event is designed to be managed by a small team of 2-3 organizers.

1. Event Overview
  • Event Name: M.U.R.A. Freedom Cup 2025
  • Club: Malayan Umamusume Racing Association (M.U.R.A.)
  • Target Audience: Malaysian and Singaporean Umamusume Pretty Derby players (in-game club members).
  • Climax/Main Event Date: August 30, 2025
  • Organizer Team Size: 2-3 people
  • Tournament Format: Triple Elimination (3v3v3 or 3v3v3v3 per race match)

1.1 Triple Elimination Format Details

The Freedom Cup utilizes a unique triple elimination structure across three brackets: A, B, and C. Refer to the table below for a detailed breakdown.

Phase Current Bracket 1st Place Action 2nd Place Action 3rd+ Place Action
Phase One Bracket Determiner Advances to Bracket A Advances to Bracket B Advances to Bracket C
Phase Two From Bracket A Remains in Bracket A Moves to Bracket B Moves to Bracket C
From Bracket B Remains in Bracket B Moves to Bracket C Moves to Bracket C
From Bracket C Remains in Bracket C Eliminated Eliminated

This process continues until a single champion emerges from each of the three brackets.

2. Event Timeline & Phases

The event is divided into three main phases, leading up to the Freedom Cup climax.

Phase Dates Objective Key Activities
Phase 1: Pre-Event Hype & Registration July 30 - August 15, 2025 Build excitement, attract participants, and establish foundational rules. Official Announcement, Rules & Format Deep Dive, Registration Period.
Phase 2: Warm-up & Seeding Races August 16 - August 25, 2025 Engage participants, provide practice opportunities, and facilitate initial player grouping. Optional "Freedom Sprint" Practice Races, Seeding Challenge.
Phase 3: Freedom Cup 2025 - The Climax August 26 - August 30, 2025 Execute the main tournament matches and crown the champions. Bracket Creation & Announcement, Execution of Matches, Finals & Winner Announcement.

2.1 Phase 1: Pre-Event Hype & Registration (July 30 - August 15, 2025)

Objective: Build excitement, attract participants, and establish foundational rules.

Activity: Official Announcement

Dates: July 30 - August 2

Create and disseminate a visually appealing announcement graphic/message across all M.U.R.A. communication channels (Discord, WhatsApp, social media). Include event name, climax date, and format.

Key Task: Draft announcement, review for clarity.

Activity: Rules & Format Deep Dive

Dates: August 3 - August 7

Develop a detailed document explaining the triple elimination format (Phase One & Two), game-specific rules (e.g., allowed Umamusume, support cards, race conditions), and any other relevant guidelines. Host a text-based Q&A session.

Key Task: Draft rules document, moderate Q&A.

Activity: Registration Period

Dates: August 8 - August 15

Set up a simple online registration form (e.g., Google Forms) to collect participant in-game IDs, contact information, and region. Implement clear deadlines and send reminders.

Key Task: Set up and monitor registration form.

2.2 Phase 2: Warm-up & Seeding Races (August 16 - August 25, 2025)

Objective: Engage participants, provide practice opportunities, and facilitate initial player grouping.

Activity: Optional "Freedom Sprint" Practice Races

Dates: August 16 - August 20

Encourage players to self-organize 3v3 practice matches and share results/screenshots in a dedicated club channel. Offer informal recognition (e.g., "bragging rights").

Key Task: Create dedicated channel, encourage participation.

Activity: Seeding Challenge

Dates: August 21 - August 25

Implement a simple challenge (e.g., single best run submission on a specific race condition) to help group players fairly for the initial triple elimination bracket.

Key Task: Set challenge parameters, collect and verify submissions.

2.3 Phase 3: Freedom Cup 2025 - The Climax (August 26 - August 30, 2025)

Objective: Execute the main tournament matches and crown the champions.

Activity: Bracket Creation & Announcement

Date: August 26

Based on registrations and seeding (if applicable), create the initial triple elimination brackets using an online bracket generator (e.g., Challonge.com). Publicly announce the brackets.

Key Task: Handle bracket setup and initial announcement.

Activity: Execution of Matches

Dates: August 27 - August 29

Players will self-organize and conduct their 3v3 matches. Results, accompanied by screenshots, must be reported in designated "match reporting" channels for each bracket (A, B, C) by strict deadlines.

Key Task: Monitor reporting channels, update brackets, manage disputes.

Activity: Freedom Cup 2025 Finals & Winner Announcement

Date: August 30

Conduct the final matches. If feasible, consider live-streaming the semi-finals and finals. Promptly verify all final results. Announce the Freedom Cup 2025 Champion and bracket winners.

Key Task: Oversee final matches, manage streaming (if applicable), verify results, announce winners.

3. Organizer Roles & Responsibilities

For a team of 2-3 organizers, clear role delegation is crucial for efficient event management.

Role Primary Responsibilities
Organizer 1: The Strategist & Main Communicator Overall event planning, timeline management, strategic oversight, primary participant contact, drafting announcements, overseeing bracket progression.
Organizer 2: The Logistics & Tech Lead Setting up registration forms, managing online bracket system, technical support (streaming/Discord), collecting and verifying match results.
Organizer 3: The Community & Hype Manager (If Available) Creating engaging content, encouraging participation, moderating community channels, assisting with live stream commentary/tech support.

*Note: If only two organizers are available, these responsibilities will be distributed between Organizer 1 and Organizer 2.

4. Key Considerations for a Small Team
  • Automation: Leverage online tools (forms, bracket generators) to minimize manual workload.
  • Clear Communication: Provide unambiguous rules, deadlines, and expectations to participants to reduce confusion and disputes.
  • Community Engagement: Empower and encourage participants to self-organize matches and report results, fostering a sense of shared responsibility.
  • Realism: Prioritize the smooth execution of the core tournament format over implementing overly complex features.
  • Contingency Planning: Develop simple plans for common issues such as player dropouts or match disputes.
  • Post-Event Feedback: Collect feedback from participants after the event to identify areas for improvement in future tournaments.

This documentation serves as a comprehensive guide for the M.U.R.A. Freedom Cup 2025, enabling a small team of organizers to deliver a successful and engaging event.
